# Sequential Thinking — Product Decisions

## Goal
Structured, reflective problem-solving for complex product decisions.

---

## Process

### Step 1: Define the Problem
- What exactly is the problem?
- What are the constraints?
- What does success look like?

### Step 2: Gather Information
- What do we know?
- What don't we know?
- What assumptions are we making?

### Step 3: Generate Options
- List all possible approaches
- Don't filter yet (quantity over quality)

### Step 4: Evaluate Options
- Pros/cons for each
- Effort/impact matrix
- Risk assessment

### Step 5: Make Decision
- Pick 1-2 options
- Document rationale
- Define next steps

### Step 6: Reflect
- Was this the right decision?
- What would we do differently?
- What did we learn?

---

## Application Modes

### Explicit (visible markers)
```
THOUGHT 1: [Initial analysis]
THOUGHT 2: [Deeper dive]
THOUGHT 3: [Consider alternatives]
THOUGHT 4: [Make decision]
THOUGHT 5: [Reflect on decision]
```

### Implicit (internal methodology)
- Apply the process without visible markers
- Use for quick decisions
