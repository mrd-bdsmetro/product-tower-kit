# Marketing Psychology — 70+ Mental Models

## Goal
Apply psychological principles to marketing and product decisions.

---

## Foundational Thinking (10 models)

1. **First Principles** — Break down to fundamentals
2. **Jobs to Be Done** — What job is the user hiring your product for?
3. **Pareto (80/20)** — 20% of effort → 80% of results
4. **Inversion** — Think backwards from failure
5. **Second-Order Thinking** — What happens after what happens?
6. **Circle of Competence** — Know what you know
7. **Margin of Safety** — Build in buffer
8. **Hanlon's Razor** — Never attribute to malice what can be explained by stupidity
9. **Occam's Razor** — Simplest explanation is usually correct
10. **Sunk Cost Fallacy** — Past cost shouldn't affect future decisions

---

## Understanding Buyers (15 models)

11. **Mere Exposure** — People prefer what they're familiar with
12. **Confirmation Bias** — People seek confirming evidence
13. **Endowment Effect** — People value what they own more
14. **IKEA Effect** — People value what they build
15. **Status Quo Bias** — People prefer current state
16. **Anchoring** — First number influences perception
17. **Framing** — How you present changes perception
18. **Social Proof** — People follow what others do
19. **Authority** — People trust experts
20. **Liking** — People buy from people they like
21. **Scarcity** — Limited availability increases desire
22. **Urgency** — Time pressure drives action
23. **Fear of Missing Out (FOMO)** — People fear missing opportunities
24. **Cognitive Dissonance** — People resolve mental conflicts
25. **Dunning-Kruger Effect** — Incompetent people overestimate ability
